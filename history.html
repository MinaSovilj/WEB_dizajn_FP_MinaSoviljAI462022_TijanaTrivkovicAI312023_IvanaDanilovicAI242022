    • History of Astrology

    Astrology is one of the oldest human traditions, dating back thousands of years. It began in ancient Mesopotamia, where Babylonian astrologers tracked celestial movements and linked them to events on Earth as early as the 2nd millennium BCE.

    The Greeks, especially during the Hellenistic period, laid much of the foundation for Western astrology. Vedic (Jyotish) astrology developed independently in India with spiritual depth and complexity.

    Timeline of Astrology

    • 2000 BCE – Babylon: Astrology begins as celestial observation linked to earthly events.
    • 4th century BCE – Greece: Formal astrology emerges. Ptolemy’s *Tetrabiblos* sets the standard.
    • 1st century BCE – India: Vedic astrology takes root as a spiritual science.
    • Islamic Golden Age: Arab scholars preserve and translate Greek works.
    • 14th–17th century – Europe: Astrology resurges during the Renaissance.
    • 20th century – Today: Psychological and pop astrology rise through media.

    Famous Astrologers

    Ptolemy

    Author of *Tetrabiblos*, foundational for Western astrology.

    Abu Ma’shar

    Persian astrologer, helped transmit Hellenistic ideas to the Islamic world.

    Nostradamus

    Famous for his prophetic writings rooted in astrological methods.
