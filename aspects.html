    • Aspects in Astrology

    In astrology, aspects are the angles formed between planets in a natal chart. They reveal how planetary energies interact, creating harmony, tension, or opportunities for growth in a person's life.

    Understanding aspects is essential for interpreting a chart because planets rarely act in isolation. The relationships between planets influence personality traits, challenges, talents, and major life events.

    Aspects are measured in degrees, with each type having its own meaning. Some aspects facilitate smooth flow and ease, while others indicate conflict or lessons to be learned.

    Major Aspects

    Conjunction (0°)

    Planets in the same sign combine their energies, creating a strong, focused influence. Can be harmonious or intense depending on planets involved.

    Sextile (60°)

    A harmonious aspect that encourages opportunities and cooperation between planets. Often brings talents and easy communication.

    Square (90°)

    A challenging aspect that creates tension, obstacles, or internal conflict. Encourages growth through effort and resolution.

    Trine (120°)

    A very harmonious aspect, promoting ease, talent, and flow of energy. Brings natural gifts and effortless cooperation between planets.

    Opposition (180°)

    Represents polarity and tension between planets. Challenges us to balance opposing energies, often through relationships or external situations.

    Minor Aspects

    Quincunx / Inconjunct (150°)

    Indicates adjustment and adaptation. Often shows areas of tension that require compromise or re-evaluation.

    Semi-Sextile (30°)

    A subtle, mild aspect that encourages awareness and small adjustments between planetary energies.

    Semi-Square (45°)

    Represents minor tension or friction, often nudging toward necessary change or growth.
